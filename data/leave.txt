EMPLOYEE LEAVE POLICY

XYZ CORPORATION

Version: 1.0
Effective Date: January 1, 2026

---

1. PURPOSE

The purpose of this Leave Policy is to provide employees with clear guidelines regarding leave entitlements, application procedures, approval processes, and responsibilities while ensuring smooth business operations.

---

2. ELIGIBILITY

This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of XYZ Corporation.

Contract employees and consultants are governed by the terms of their respective contracts and may not be eligible for all leave benefits described in this policy.

---

3. TYPES OF LEAVE

3.1 Annual Leave

Employees are entitled to 24 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.

Key Rules:

* Leave is credited monthly.
* Employees may carry forward up to 10 unused leave days to the next calendar year.
* Any leave balance exceeding the carry-forward limit will expire at the end of the year.
* Annual leave requests should be submitted at least 5 working days in advance whenever possible.

---

3.2 Sick Leave

Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year.

Key Rules:

* Sick leave may be used when an employee is unable to work due to illness or injury.
* A medical certificate may be required for absences longer than 3 consecutive working days.
* Unused sick leave cannot be carried forward to the next year.

---

3.3 Casual Leave

Employees are entitled to 6 days of casual leave per year.

Key Rules:

* Casual leave may be used for personal emergencies or urgent matters.
* Casual leave should generally not exceed 2 consecutive working days without manager approval.
* Unused casual leave expires at the end of the calendar year.

---

3.4 Maternity Leave

Eligible employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave.

Requirements:

* Employees should notify the Human Resources department at least 8 weeks before the expected leave start date whenever possible.
* Medical documentation may be requested.

---

3.5 Paternity Leave

Eligible employees are entitled to 10 working days of paid paternity leave.

Requirements:

* Leave must be taken within 3 months of the child's birth or adoption.
* Supporting documentation may be requested.

---

3.6 Bereavement Leave

Employees are entitled to 5 working days of paid bereavement leave in the event of the death of an immediate family member.

Immediate family includes:

* Spouse
* Parent
* Child
* Sibling
* Legal guardian

Additional leave may be granted at management discretion.

---

3.7 Unpaid Leave

Employees may request unpaid leave when all available paid leave balances have been exhausted.

Approval is subject to:

* Business requirements
* Management approval
* Human Resources review

Unpaid leave may impact benefits and salary calculations where applicable.

---

4. PUBLIC HOLIDAYS

Employees are entitled to all company-recognized public holidays.

If business requirements necessitate working on a public holiday, employees may receive:

* Compensatory time off, or
* Additional compensation as determined by company policy.

---

5. LEAVE APPLICATION PROCESS

Employees must follow the steps below when requesting leave:

Step 1:
Submit a leave request through the company's Employee Management System.

Step 2:
Provide the leave type, start date, end date, and reason for leave.

Step 3:
The reporting manager reviews the request.

Step 4:
The manager approves or rejects the request.

Step 5:
The employee receives notification of the decision.

For emergency leave situations, employees should notify their manager as soon as reasonably possible.

---

6. LEAVE APPROVAL GUIDELINES

Managers will consider:

* Team workload
* Business requirements
* Staffing availability
* Employee leave balance

Approval is not guaranteed and may be adjusted based on operational needs.

---

7. LEAVE DURING PROBATION

Employees serving a probation period may take:

* Sick leave
* Emergency leave

Annual leave during probation requires approval from both the reporting manager and Human Resources department.

---

8. LEAVE BALANCE MANAGEMENT

Employees can view their leave balances through the Employee Management System.

Human Resources is responsible for maintaining accurate leave records.

Employees are encouraged to regularly review their leave balances and report discrepancies promptly.

---

9. MISUSE OF LEAVE

Providing false information, misrepresenting leave reasons, or abusing leave privileges may result in disciplinary action, including termination of employment.

---

10. RETURN TO WORK

Employees returning from extended leave may be required to:

* Meet with their manager
* Provide supporting medical documentation when applicable
* Complete any required return-to-work procedures

---

11. POLICY EXCEPTIONS

Any exception to this policy requires written approval from Human Resources and senior management.

---

12. POLICY REVIEW

XYZ Corporation reserves the right to amend, modify, suspend, or terminate this policy at any time to meet business, legal, or operational requirements.

Employees will be notified of significant policy changes.
